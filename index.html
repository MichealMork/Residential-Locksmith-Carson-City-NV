High-Security Locks & Door Reinforcement Tactics

Standard builder-grade locks are often defeated by bump keys or simple drilling within minutes. High-security locks offer restricted keyways (keys can’t be duplicated without authorization), hardened steel inserts, and complex sidebar mechanisms. Pair these with heavy-duty strike plates secured by 3-inch screws that anchor into the home’s structural framing, and you dramatically boost resistance against kick-ins. A residential locksmith can also install wrap-around plates and reinforce door jambs, turning your entryways into formidable barriers.

Maintenance Tips to Extend Lock Lifespan

Simple annual maintenance can double the life of your locks. Use graphite powder (never oil-based lubricants) in keyways to prevent sticking. Check alignment of deadbolts with strike plates—if a latch doesn’t extend smoothly, door sag or weather shifting may be at play. For smart locks, replace batteries every 6–12 months and update firmware to patch security vulnerabilities. A quick call to your locksmith for a tune-up can prevent inconvenient failures.
